About Healthy After 50

Healthy After 50 is a practical, no-nonsense blog for Americans navigating Medicare, Social Security, retirement income, and staying independent at home.

Most retirement and health information online is either too vague to be useful or buried in government jargon that takes an hour to decode. This blog exists to fix that.

What We Cover

  • Medicare: Part A, Part B, Part D, Medigap, Medicare Advantage — explained in plain English with real numbers, not approximations.
  • Retirement Planning: Social Security timing, RMD rules, IRA and 401(k) decisions, estate basics like wills and beneficiary designations.
  • Aging in Place: Home safety upgrades, mobility aids, medical alert systems, and the practical decisions that help people stay in their own homes longer.
  • Senior Health: Managing chronic conditions, prescription costs, nutrition, and the health topics that matter most after 50.

Our Approach

Every article is written with specific dollar amounts, real deadlines, and honest acknowledgment of where the rules are complicated. We don't round numbers. We don't pretend decisions are simpler than they are. And we always point you to the official government sources — SSA.gov, Medicare.gov — so you can verify what you read here.

The content on this blog reflects current federal program rules and is updated regularly to reflect legislative changes, including updates under the Inflation Reduction Act and SECURE 2.0.

Important Disclaimer

Nothing on Healthy After 50 constitutes legal, financial, or medical advice. The information here is for educational purposes only. Before making any decision about Medicare, Social Security, your retirement accounts, or your health, please consult a licensed professional who can review your specific situation.
